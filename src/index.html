Deep Time - An Incredibly Long Scroll through Time

Reading Peter Brannen's excellent piece Rambling through Time on how poorly people perceive how old the earth really is. The earth, from a human perspective is unimaginably ancient. Dinosaurs have been gone for approximately 66 million years, a number so large that it's basically meaningless to a person. Inspired by Josh Worth's wonderful visualization If the Moon Were Only 1 Pixel , I thought it might be useful to try a pixel accurate view of the history of the earth. Now, you can scroll through the history of the Earth to get a flavor of just how old it is. In the visualization one pixel equals 500 years. For reference one pixel roughly equals 0.26 millimeters or 0.016 inches, depending on your screen resolution.

Note: Best viewed using ↑ ↓ buttons or touch scroll, depending on your device. If you find yourself growing impatient you can use the dropdown menus to jump to specific events.
